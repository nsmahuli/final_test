Spring Movies

Pride & Prejudice (2005)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Drama · Rating: PG

Elizabeth Bennet and her sisters navigate love, family expectations, and social class when the mysterious Mr. Darcy enters their lives, leading Elizabeth to question her first impressions.

Why it feels like spring: Misty fields, green hills, and feelings that open up slowly make the whole movie feel like a rainy countryside walk where everything is just starting to bloom.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a lightly sweet orgeat with a warm bowl of Mr. Bingley's white soup, inspired by the elegant Regency setting and spirited charm of Pride and Prejudice.

Source: IMDb

About Time (2013)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Fantasy, Drama · Rating: R

A young man learns he can travel back through moments in his life, using the ability to shape his relationships, fall in love, and understand what really matters.

Why it feels like spring: The story keeps circling back to starting over and trying again, like emotional spring cleaning where you gently rearrange your life.

Food / Drink Pairing: A classic English Breakfast Tea with warm British Afternoon Tea Scones,inspired by the cozy family scenes and gentle romantic tone of About Time.

Source: IMDb

Notting Hill (1999)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Comedy · Rating: PG-13

A quiet London bookstore owner’s life changes when a world-famous actress unexpectedly steps into his shop, sparking an unlikely relationship complicated by fame and everyday life.

Why it feels like spring: Flower stalls, soft rain, and a gentle, hopeful tone make it feel like walking around a city that’s just starting to feel warm again.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a cozy Earl Grey tea with warm buttered crumpets, inspired by the charming London streets and romantic atmosphere of Notting Hill.

Source: IMDb

La La Land (2016)

Type: Movie · Genre: Musical, Romance, Drama · Rating: PG-13

An aspiring actress and a passionate jazz musician fall for each other while pursuing their dreams in Los Angeles, facing the tension between love and ambition.

Why it feels like spring: Evening skies, first dates, and the buzzing feeling that your life might be about to start give it a hopeful, “new chapter” energy.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a dreamy lavender latte with delicate French macarons, inspired by the glowing jazz clubs, vintage charm, and starry Los Angeles nights of La La Land.

Source: IMDb

Emma. (2020)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama · Rating: PG

A confident young woman in a small English town loves arranging matches for others, but her plans often unravel, forcing her to confront her own romantic misunderstandings.

Why it feels like spring: Garden walks, pastel outfits, and picnics make the film feel like a fancy early-spring garden party with just a little chaos.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a soothing cup of chamomile tea with warm currant buns, inspired by the gentle sweetness and heartfelt warmth of Emma.

Source: IMDb

Spring TV Shows

Anne with an E (2017–2019)

Type: TV Series · Genre: Period Drama · Rating: TV-PG

A bright and imaginative orphan girl finds a new home on Prince Edward Island, where she builds friendships, navigates challenges, and reshapes her identity.

Why it feels like spring: Open skies, fields, and awkward but exhilarating moments of growing into yourself match the mood of a season that’s all about renewal.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a sweet glass of raspberry cordial with soft tea biscuits, inspired by the cozy farmhouse warmth and imaginative spirit of Anne with an E.
