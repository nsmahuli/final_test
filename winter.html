Winter Movies

Little Women (2019)

Type: Movie · Genre: Drama, Romance · Rating: PG

The March sisters — Jo, Meg, Beth, and Amy — grow up, drift apart, and circle back together as they chase art, love, and independence.

Why it feels like winter: Snowy scenes, Christmas mornings, and candlelit interiors make it feel like crawling under a quilt with your family.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a warm cup of black tea with soft slices of old-fashioned gingerbread, inspired by the cozy New England winters, sisterly warmth, and heartfelt March-family traditions of Little Women.

Source: IMDb

Home Alone (1990)

Type: Movie · Genre: Family, Comedy · Rating: PG

Eight-year-old Kevin is accidentally left behind when his family flies to Paris for Christmas and has to protect his house from two burglars using over-the-top traps.

Why it feels like winter: Suburban Christmas lights, snow, and that very specific holiday chaos make this feel like childhood December.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a cold glass of Coca-Cola with a classic cheese pizza, inspired by the mischievous fun, cozy holiday chaos, and iconic “a lovely cheese pizza, just for me” moment of Home Alone.

Source: IMDb

The Polar Express (2004)

Type: Movie · Genre: Animation, Fantasy, Family · Rating: G

On Christmas Eve, a boy who’s starting to doubt Santa boards a mysterious train heading to the North Pole and learns about belief and bravery along the way.

Why it feels like winter: Snow-covered tracks, glowing windows, and a whole song about hot chocolate make this extremely holiday-coded.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a warm cup of rich hot chocolate with a sweet holiday sugar cookie, inspired by the magical train ride, festive cheer, and unforgettable “Hot Chocolate!” scene of The Polar Express.

Source: IMDb

Love Actually (2003)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama · Rating: R

Multiple London love stories — messy, sweet, awkward, and sad — collide in the weeks leading up to Christmas.

Why it feels like winter: Office parties, school pageants, and airport reunions are wrapped in Christmas music and fake snow.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a festive glass of mulled wine with a cozy slice of classic fruitcake, inspired by the heartwarming holiday moments, London charm, and intertwined love stories of Love Actually.

Source: IMDb

Frozen (2013)

Type: Movie · Genre: Animation, Adventure, Family · Rating: PG

Sisters Elsa and Anna grapple with Elsa’s ice powers, a kingdom stuck in winter, and what real love actually looks like.

Why it feels like winter: Eternal snow, ice palaces, and sparkly frost — the entire movie is one long snow day.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a frosty glass of winter lemonade with a cozy Norwegian cinnamon bun, inspired by the snowy magic, sisterly bond, and whimsical Arendelle charm of Frozen.

Source: IMDb

The Holiday (2006)

Type: Movie · Genre: Romance, Comedy · Rating: PG-13

Two women swap homes for the holidays — one in a snowy English village, one in sunny LA — and unexpectedly find new love and new ways of living.

Why it feels like winter: Cozy cottages, fireplaces, and quiet days hiding from your normal life feel very “between Christmas and New Year’s.”

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a warm mug of creamy hot chocolate with a comforting shepherd’s pie, inspired by the cozy English cottage nights, heartfelt romance, and festive winter charm of The Holiday.

Source: IMDb

Winter TV Shows

Stranger Things (2016–2025)

Type: TV Series · Genre: Drama, Fantasy, Horror · Rating: TV-14

In the small town of Hawkins, a young boy’s disappearance unravels a series of eerie events involving secret experiments, terrifying creatures, and a girl with extraordinary abilities. The group of friends caught in the middle must work together as the mystery deepens.

Why it feels like winter: Snow-covered streets, holiday lights, and chilling supernatural moments give the early seasons a cold, atmospheric edge — the kind of suspense that feels perfect for a bundled-up winter night.

Food / Drink Pairing: Enjoy a vanilla milkshake with a stack of Eggo waffles, inspired by the nostalgic small-town diners, supernatural mysteries, and iconic Eleven moments of Stranger Things.

Source: IMDb
